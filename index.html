Moving away from an industry of the Privileged Few.

Itâ€™s no secret that the prohibition of cannabis disproportionately and adversely impacted people of color. To counter this, many states and cities have implemented social equity programs in connection with the legalization of medical or adult-use cannabis. Social equity deals with justice and fairness within social policy. Trying to ensure that people of color, and those with marijuana offenses prior to legalization, be afforded an opportunity to participate, meaningfully, in this burgeoning industry.

The demand for social equity is currently very high in the industry and has been heightened by recent events: the mobilization of the Black Lives Matter movement, nationwide protests against police brutality, and the like.

It is not enough to just hire, we must empower

We recognize that we must do more. We created SocialEquity.org to be dedicated to advocating for Black, People of Color and LGBTQ employment candidates, clients, and employees. Our intention is to challenge the industry and help address the injustices each of us witnesses every day.

We active work with those that are taking steps to advance the descheduling and/or legalization of marijuana. It is well documented that marijuana has been a key driver of mass criminalization in this country and hundreds of thousands of people, the majority of whom are Black or Latina/o, have their lives impacted by these arrests each year.
